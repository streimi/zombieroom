# Zombieroom

## General Info
[Zombieroom](https://www.ustp.at/de/stories/wie-ein-escape-room-data-science-lehrbar-macht-der-zombieroom) is an award-winning Educational Escape Room created by Thomas Delissen, a professor at the University of Applied Sciences (FH) St. Pölten in Austria.

Rather than just being a scary game, it is a sophisticated gamification project designed for students in Data Science and Business Analytics.


## Concept
The project immerses students in a fictional zombie apocalypse in Austria. They take on the roles of the "Data Driven Disease Defence Department" (D5-Team). Their mission is to stop the outbreak using the technical skills they’ve learned in their degree.


## How it Works
Students spend three days working through various data-related challenges to save the country:
- Data Analysis & Algorithms: Teams must analyze the spread of the virus and develop a cure.
- Visualization: They use data visualization techniques to track the epidemic and decide where to distribute resources.
- Simulations: Each phase of the game ends with an "Epidemic Simulation." The computer evaluates the team's data-driven decisions and determines the outcome - ranging from a mild containment to a total national collapse.


## Recognition
Thomas Delissen received the Prize for Innovative Higher Education Teaching (Ars Docendi or similar regional honors) for this project. It is highly regarded because it bridges the gap between dry theoretical data science and high-stakes, real-world application (even if the "world" involves zombies).
